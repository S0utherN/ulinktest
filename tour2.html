Multi-Day Tour in the UK

Day 1-3: Kick off your tour in London, with visits to key attractions including the Big Ben, Buckingham Palace, James Park, Royal Cavalry Barracks, British Museum, London Eye, Oxford Street, St. Paul's Cathedral, Tower of London, and the Greenwich Observatory.

Day 4: Head to Cambridge, where you'll explore the Cambridge Museum, Mathematical Bridge, and King's College.

Day 5: Tour the splendid Windsor Castle.

Day 6: Experience the breathtaking views of the Seven Sisters cliffs.

Day 7: A day for leisure.

Day 8: The journey takes you to Oxford, where you will explore the Oxford University Museum, Christ Church, Bridge of Sighs, and Bodleian Library.

Day 9: Head to the Cotswolds, visiting the beautiful villages of Bibury and Bourton-on-the-Water.

Day 10: Visit the city of Bath, where you will explore the ancient Roman Baths, Bath Cathedral, Royal Crescent, and enjoy the city's nighttime views.

Day 11: Explore the city of Cardiff and the stunning Cardiff Bay in Wales.

Day 12: A day for leisure.

Day 13: In Manchester, tour Old Trafford Stadium, the Football Museum, and Shakespeare's birthplace.

Day 14: Visit York, exploring York Castle, York Walls, York Minster, and The Shambles.

Day 15: Head to Whitby, the birthplace of the famed Dracula, visiting Whitby Abbey.

Day 16: Enjoy the beautiful landscapes of Lake District, visiting Windermere Lake and the world of Beatrix Potter.

Day 17-18: Journey to Scotland to explore the city of Edinburgh, with visits to Edinburgh Castle, the Royal Mile, the Scottish National Gallery, Princes Street, Princes Street Gardens, Holyrood Palace, and Calton Hill.

Day 19-20: Explore the beautiful landscapes of Scotland, including Loch Ness, Glenfinnan Viaduct, and the Isle of Skye with Neist Point Lighthouse, ending the Scottish adventure at Fort William.

Buckingham Palace

Buckingham Palace, a jewel of British history, serves as the London residence and administrative headquarters for the UK's monarch. Known for its regal grandeur, it captivates visitors with its iconic Changing of the Guard ceremony. Within the palace's opulent state rooms, one can admire a rich collection of fine art and elegant furnishings, offering a glimpse into the royal lifestyle.

Hyde Park

Hyde Park, one of the largest parks in London, is a true sanctuary within the bustling city. It offers a tranquil escape with its vast green spaces, serene lakes, and beautiful ornamental gardens. From leisurely strolls to boating on the Serpentine lake, or attending concerts and events at the open-air theatre, there's no shortage of recreational activities here. This urban oasis is a perfect spot for relaxation, exercise, or cultural exploration.

Westminster Abbey

Westminster Abbey stands as a magnificent testament to over a thousand years of British history. As a place of coronations, royal weddings, and burials, it holds a special place in the heart of the nation. The Abbey's stunning Gothic architecture and its wealth of artistic treasures, including medieval stained glass, ornate stone carvings, and intricate tombs, draw in millions of visitors each year. It's not just a place of worship but also an unforgettable journey through British history and culture.
